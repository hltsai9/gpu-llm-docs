# Part 8 — Creative Workflows

The rest of this guide is written for people who build LLM systems. Part 8 is written for people who use them — writers, designers, marketers, producers, editors. You don't need most of the plumbing. You do need enough mental model to collaborate with the tools without being steered around by them.

<div class="hero" markdown>

The pitfalls in creative work with AI are different from the pitfalls in engineering. Engineers worry about hallucinated citations and prompt injection. Creatives worry about generic-sounding drafts, lost voice, and the slow erosion of taste that comes from accepting "fine" output too often. This part is about the craft of using the tool without being used by it.

</div>

## Who wrote this part and why

This part is shorter and more opinionated than the rest. The field is evolving; the specific tools will change; the skills won't. What we hope you take from it is a *way of working* rather than a list of features.

## The one sentence to take away

AI tools compress the mechanical parts of creative work, which leaves more of your time for the parts that are actually creative — but only if you actively defend the parts that are actually creative, because the tools will happily fill them in with averages.
